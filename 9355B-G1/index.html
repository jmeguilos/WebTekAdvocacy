Drugs.

Inhaled, swallowed, injected; originally concocted to serve medicinal purposes towards those afflicted with sickness, mankind's drive for discovery has led to the creation of substances that cure both physical and mental diseases.

However, relief, as short-lasting as it is given, can drive one to addiction. Surely, one would always want comfort in their lowest of lows.

The overuse and abuse of drugs can lead to an addiction: an addiction that not only disillusions one's sense of reality but can also cause the devastation of one's very life.

Alcohol.

Liquor, spirits, drink; alcohol has always been imbibed and served amongst people. Fermented, distilled; different forms with different qualities and strength.

In small doses, alcohol is a stimulant, letting lose one's stress and providing one a sense of short-term but great relief.

However, in large doses, alcohol becomes a poison, a depressant that slows down the brain and the nervous system, leading to sluggish and rash actions.

Tobacco.

Tobacco. Cigarettes, smoking, snuff; found and used in our daily lives, tobacco is perhaps one of the most common forms of vice. Highly stimulating with every whiff and giving the user a buzz of pleasure and energy, adrenaline to the senses.

Tobacco has found itself as a common product, a commodity and smoking as a daily routine.

The effect lingers for a short while and the user is left with wanting more. Chemicals from the nicotine can stay for as long as six weeks and overuse of the product can lead the brain to addiction, wanting more.

Prevention.

"Prevention need not be impossible."

Abuse of substances can lead to bad decisions and addiction. Recovering can be difficult for a lot of people and there are internal and external factors to take in but prevention is indeed doable. Community-based prevention programs present everywhere, even in your local community.

If you are STRESSED or DEPRESSED, find coping methods like exercise, meditation or groups in your local community. SEEK OUT therapists and counseling, and converse with people you’re comfortable with. FIND a lifestyle that suites you, hobbies that entertain you. PARTAKE in activities, sport, relationships. THINK about people or things you care about and enjoy company.

Rehabilitation.

“Addiction need not be a life sentence.”

In the medical field, addiction is considered a chronic and progressive brain disease. Addiction CAN be cured. Or at the very least, managed and controlled. WITHDRAWAL is the anxiety one feels when starting to curb addiction and while it may be difficult to maintain, one should stick to treatment to prevent RELAPSE. TREATMENTS address the person as a whole and there are a number of THERAPIES to check out.

Behavioral Therapy helps people with managing situations to avoid substance abuse.

Contingency Management applies reinforcement and stimuli control via feedback to help change a person’s behavior.

Motivational Enhancement Therapy is about techniques applied to quickly evoke quick motivational change as opposed to the general step by step recovery process.

Family Therapy is youth friendly and approaches substance abuse recovery by introspective outlook one’s own familial circumstances and also encourages other members to act and help.
